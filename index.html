How to Survive at the End of the World

By RC Joshua

A LitRPG mixing time magic, humor, fun weapon design, and a unique and interesting class.

When an accident kicks Sean Lawrence forward in time, he finds himself square in the apocalyptic end-times. The world is doomed, and he only has a mysterious guidebook, a leather jacket, and a home-made knife to keep him company. He could spend his days grinding low-level mobs, gathering rat pelts, and waiting for a chance to escape.

Or he could rise up, slay dragons, and fight against the odds. Faced with a choice between the dead-end life he’s been living and a possibility of greatness, Sean has to decide if he’s willing to risk his life and more for a chance to save Earth itself.

Reviews

Different from Deadworld, but just as Good

R.C. has proven beyond a reasonable doubt that Deadworld Isekai was not a fluke – the man writes the good words.

A clueless but earnest protagonist, Sean offers a believable window into how an average person would react when thrust into a system-apocalypse style future. Almost immediately, we the reader are immersed in a strange new world with little more explanation into how things work than the MC.

The story is only in its infancy but it has all the ingredients to make it an enjoyable long term read. I can’t judge what hasn’t been published yet, but having read the author’s other work I feel comfortable saying that if you’re enjoying the story by chapter 10, you’ll continue to do so. If you don’t, it might not be for you.

Just a few chapters in and I’m hooked!

As a fan of this writer’s other novel, I have to say that this is looking like a winner, too! Savvy self-aware protagonist; interesting world-building, situations, and challenges; clever, high-quality writing … what’s not to like? Too often, characters in web novels are just not that smart — like the horror movie red shirt walking alone into the basement, the hero is mostly just carrying the idiot ball. But just eight chapters in, I can tell that this story features a thoughtful, rational hero who does not make me yell in protest at his poor life choices. I’m looking forward to more.

The only book that does "junk crafting" right!

I like to tool around in my worshop and take trash to create stuff that looks awful, but works. I've looked for years for a story that captured that feeling and method of building things in an action packed fantasy world, but to no success. Until I found this book. The story is excellent, the character is a kind but practical man, and the crafting and powers he gets are out of this world hilarious. I feel like I'm sitting in my garage with duct tape and hot glue working alongside him every time I read a chapter. Do yourself a favor and give it a read.
